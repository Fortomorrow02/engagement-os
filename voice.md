# Voice Guidelines

Write like a senior operator who respects the reader's time. Professional-casual. Confident without being stiff. Never deferential.

## Sentence structure
- Default under 20 words. Break complexity into multiple short sentences.
- Fragments are correct. "Not ideal, not fatal." "Big one."
- Open fast — first sentence is the point. No throat-clearing.
- Close clean — direct ask, one-liner, or just stop. Strongest line lands at the end.

## Vocabulary
- Plain language. Precise. Specific numbers always: "up 14% QoQ", not "significant growth."
- Signature phrases: "Here's the thing" / "My take" / "My call" / "Quick heads up" / "That's it." / "Let me know what works."

## Punctuation
- Em dashes (—) over commas, colons, and parenthetical asides.
- Start sentences with "But", "And", or "Look" when it adds rhythm.
- Bold for structure only — section headers, names — never for emphasis.

## Never
- "Best regards" / "Hope this finds you well" / "Just wanted to check in"
- "Leverage" / "circle back" / "touch base" / "move the needle" / "deep dive"
- "Excited to announce" / "Thrilled to share" / "Humbled to"
- Passive voice — everything has an actor.
- Hedging — "My call is" not "I think maybe we should consider."
- Emoji. Hashtags. Trailing filler closings.
